# UX Scorecard

Client: Twin Cities Contracting Services Inc.
Website: https://twincitiescontractingservices.com/
Reviewed: 2026-07-16

## Summary

The current site has credible business substance: local roofing history since 1964, clear services, phone visibility, service-area depth, and structured data. The modernization opportunity is to make those strengths easier to scan and act on from a phone.

## Current Site

### Strengths

- Clear local roofing specialization.
- Phone number is visible and repeated.
- Strong longevity claim: since 1964.
- Bloomington address and broad Twin Cities service area.
- Service depth and FAQ content support SEO.
- Public project/service imagery is available.

### Opportunities

- Mobile hero headline is long and overflows in the captured 390px screenshot.
- First screen is more SEO-heavy than action-focused.
- CTA path can be clarified into call now plus inspection request.
- Trust proof can be moved higher and made more scannable.
- Services can be summarized before long-form content.

## Concept Improvements

- Shorter first-screen headline: "Roofing since 1964."
- Phone CTA and free inspection CTA above the fold.
- Proof strip immediately after hero.
- Four core service cards.
- Project imagery used as trust proof.
- Demo form clearly labeled and not connected.
- Mobile sticky call/inspection actions.

## Production Considerations

- Verify final service claims before launch.
- Preserve or redirect current SEO/service-area URLs.
- Recreate `RoofingContractor` schema using verified business details.
- Connect form only after destination, consent language, and spam handling are confirmed.
- Keep the current site live until replacement is approved.
