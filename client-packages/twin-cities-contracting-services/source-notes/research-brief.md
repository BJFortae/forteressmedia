# Research Brief

Client: Twin Cities Contracting Services Inc.
Website: https://twincitiescontractingservices.com/
Vertical: residential roofing
Location: Bloomington, MN
Phone: (952) 405-6201

## Business Context

Twin Cities Contracting Services appears to be a real local roofing contractor serving Bloomington and the broader Twin Cities metro. The current site emphasizes:

- Roofing the Twin Cities since 1964.
- Roof replacement, roof installation, roof inspection, and residential shingle roofing.
- High-quality work at reasonable prices.
- Free inspections / estimate-style phone CTA.
- Service coverage across many Twin Cities suburbs.

## Visitor Intent

Most mobile visitors are likely homeowners who need one of three things:

- Urgent or near-term roof help after damage, leaking, age, or storm concerns.
- A quote for replacement or installation.
- Confidence that the contractor is local, experienced, credible, and easy to call.

The site should make these actions easy within seconds:

- Call now.
- Request a free roof inspection or estimate.
- Verify services and service area.
- See proof of experience and trust.

## Conversion Goal

Primary: phone call or free inspection/estimate request.

Secondary: confirm service fit by roof replacement, installation, inspection, storm damage, and shingle roofing.

## Current Strengths

- Strong local longevity: since 1964.
- Simple phone CTA repeated across the site.
- Clear roofing specialization.
- Bloomington address and broad metro service footprint.
- Service pages and FAQs create SEO depth.
- Public imagery and logo are available for a credible concept.

## Current Gaps / Opportunity

- The mobile hero headline is long and overflows the viewport in a fresh 390px capture.
- The first screen reads as SEO-first rather than homeowner-action-first.
- The CTA could be clearer as "Call" plus "Request Free Inspection" rather than only a stylized phone block.
- Trust proof is available but not organized into a quick scan band.
- Service information is deep but could be structured into short cards for replacement, installation, inspection, and storm response.
- Visual polish could be made more current while preserving the established local identity.

## Competitive / Category Patterns To Match

For residential roofing, a modern small-business site should usually include:

- Above-the-fold phone CTA and estimate/inspection CTA.
- Strong local service-area signal.
- Trust proof near the top: years in business, reviews, licensed/insured if supported, workmanship/material quality.
- Clear service cards.
- Storm damage / insurance help if supported by client.
- Project/gallery proof.
- Simple estimate form with low friction.
- Sticky mobile call action.

## Proposed Prototype Direction

Build a private homepage concept that keeps the company's established local trust but modernizes the flow:

1. Compact header with logo, phone CTA, and service-area cue.
2. Hero: "Bloomington roofing backed by 60+ years in the Twin Cities" with call and inspection CTA.
3. Trust band: since 1964, local Bloomington office, residential roofing specialists, reasonable pricing.
4. Service cards: roof replacement, roof installation, roof inspection, shingle roofing.
5. Why homeowners choose them: on-time, quoted price, high-quality materials, customer service.
6. Project/gallery strip using public site imagery.
7. Service area section.
8. Estimate/contact section with phone-first CTA and non-working demo form.

## Tone

Clear, established, local, practical, and homeowner-friendly. Avoid a luxury/remodeler feel. This should feel like a reliable Twin Cities roofing contractor that has modernized the online experience without losing its local identity.
