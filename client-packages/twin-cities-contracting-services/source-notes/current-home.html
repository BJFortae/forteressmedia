Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) about Our Roofing Service

  • What factors can affect the lifespan of my roof?

     The lifespan of a roof is influenced by many factors that can vary greatly depending on materials, design, and environmental conditions. One of the most critical elements is the quality of the roofing material used. Choosing a high-quality material suited for the climate can significantly extend a roof’s longevity.

    Exposure to harsh weather conditions, such as high winds, hail, intense sunlight, snow, and ice, all accelerate wear and tear. For instance, UV radiation degrades roofing materials over time, while storm damage causes immediate problems. The presence of overhanging trees fosters algal growth and necessitates regular cleaning. Additionally, fluctuating temperatures can cause materials to expand and contract, contributing to premature aging. By considering these factors, homeowners can take steps to mitigate their impact and enhance the longevity of their roofs.

    Another crucial factor affecting a roof’s lifespan is the expertise of the roofing contractor doing the installation. Even the best materials can fail prematurely if not installed correctly. Hiring experienced professionals who adhere to manufacturer specifications and local building codes is essential.

    If it’s time to replace your roof, contact Twin Cities Contracting Services In Bloomington, Minnesota. You can depend on us to get the job done right, on time, and for the agreed price.

  • What are the signs that my roof needs repair or roof replacement?

    Knowing when to replace your roof is crucial for maintaining the integrity of your home. Several key indicators may signal it’s time for a roof replacement. Look for signs such as missing, curling, or damaged shingles, and excessive granule loss. Check for leaks or water stains on ceilings and walls inside your home, as these may indicate roof failure or damage. Furthermore, if your roof is nearing the end of its expected lifespan it’s wise to start considering a roof replacement, regardless of visible signs. Lastly, it’s a good idea to consult with a professional roofing contractor so you can make an informed decision.

    Additionally, living in the Twin Cities area means watching for regional threats. If the Twin Cities metro area just experienced a major storm, look for dark round spots on your shingles, bruising, or excessive granules filling up your gutters. In Minnesota, you generally have one to two years from the storm date to file an insurance claim, so getting an inspection right away is critical.

    If you notice heavy ice buildup along your gutters every winter, it’s a sign that your roof system may be failing. When we replace a roof in Bloomington, we install a heavy-duty ice and water shield that extends at least 24 inches past your home’s interior wall line to permanently stop these winter leaks.

    Fortunately, Minnesota state law protects you from being dropped by your insurance company for filing an “Act of God” storm claim, making it safe to address these signs of damage immediately before they cause structural rot. So if you notice any of these signs, don’t hesitate to contact Twin Cities Contracting Services Inc. for a thorough assessment.

  • How long does a typical roof installation take?

    The time needed for a roof installation depends on various factors, including the size of your home, the type of materials used for the roof replacement, and the weather conditions. Generally, an average size project can be completed within a day. The process includes the removal of the old materials, any repairs that may be necessary to the underlying structure, and the installation of the new roofing materials.
