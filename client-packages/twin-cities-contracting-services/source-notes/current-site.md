## UX / Modernization Opportunities

- Mobile hero text overflows the right edge on a 390x844 viewport. The issue affects at least the long headline lines, including "Roofing Contractor..." and "Twin Cities Contracting...".
- The first screen asks the hero headline to carry SEO, location, company name, service type, and CTA all at once. A shorter conversion-focused headline would scan better.
- The logo and hamburger are centered over the hero image, but the contact path is less immediate than it could be.
- The CTA is visible, but it sits below a long hero headline. It could be paired with a second estimate/inspection CTA and a phone CTA.
- Navigation/content repeats in the raw text, suggesting a heavy page structure that may feel crowded on mobile.
- The business has real trust proof, but it is scattered: since 1964, reasonable pricing, thousands of satisfied customers, free inspections, and customer service can become a tighter proof band.

## Recommended Pitch Framing

Lead with respect for the existing business:

"You already have the trust signals homeowners care about: roofing since 1964, a clear local phone number, and focused residential services. The opportunity is to make that credibility easier to see and act on from a phone."

Avoid over-claiming that the site is broken. The stronger pitch is modernization/uplift: cleaner mobile-first layout, stronger quote path, clearer service proof, and a more current first impression.
