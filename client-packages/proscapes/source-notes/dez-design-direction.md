# Dez Design Direction

## Audience

Design first for commercial property managers, HOA managers, facilities teams, retail/industrial site operators, and municipal buyers in the Twin Cities. They are not shopping for decorative inspiration first; they need a reliable year-round maintenance vendor who can reduce operational risk, communicate clearly, and respond during weather events.

Residential visitors still matter, but should read as a secondary path after the commercial/facility maintenance story is clear.

## UX Priorities

- Make the first screen immediately answer: "Can ProScapes reliably maintain my property?"
- Separate commercial maintenance and snow/ice needs from residential design/build browsing.
- Put proof before polish: since 1958, 24/7/365 availability, communication software, association/membership credibility, and client portal access.
- Keep mobile layout tight and resilient; current hero text clipping is the clearest visible failure to fix.
- Use direct CTA language that matches buyer intent instead of generic "Get Started."
- Make phone contact and quote request easy from every major section.

## Visual Direction

Modern, grounded, operational, and local. Avoid luxury garden, lifestyle resort, or overly decorative lawn-care styling. The site should feel like a dependable facilities partner that owns trucks, crews, weather response, communication, and repeat service quality.

Use a professional green/charcoal/white base with restrained bright green accents. Pair clean sans-serif type with practical spacing, high contrast, and strong mobile legibility. Photography should show real crews, maintained commercial properties, snow/ice work, equipment, and finished grounds. If current photography is weak, use image placement that can be swapped later without changing the layout.

## Above The Fold

Recommended structure:

1. Compact header with logo, Commercial Services, Snow & Ice, Residential, Careers, Client Portal, and a prominent quote CTA.
2. Hero image or image-backed band showing commercial grounds/equipment, not a generic scenic lawn.
3. H1: "Commercial Grounds Maintenance & Snow Response in the Twin Cities"
4. Supporting copy: emphasize year-round property care, 24/7/365 responsiveness, and clear communication.
5. Primary CTA: "Request Commercial Quote"
6. Secondary CTA: "Snow & Ice Services"
7. Utility CTA/link: "Client Portal" in header or top utility area, not competing with the lead CTA.
8. Proof strip directly below hero: "Since 1958", "24/7/365 Response", "Client Portal", "Association Member" or "Commercial + Residential Crews."

## CTA Hierarchy

- Primary: Request Commercial Quote
- Secondary: Snow & Ice Services
- Tertiary: Call ProScapes
- Utility: Client Portal
- Secondary audience: Residential Project Inquiry
- Talent path: View Careers

Avoid multiple equal-weight buttons in the hero. Commercial quote should be the obvious next step; snow/ice can be visually close because it is urgent and high-intent.

## Homepage Sections

1. Hero + proof strip
2. Commercial services overview organized by buyer need:
   - Grounds Maintenance
   - Snow & Ice Management
   - Irrigation
   - Landscape Enhancements
   - Land Clearing / Tree Removal
3. "Built for property managers" proof section with response, communication, service history, and recurring maintenance framing.
4. Snow and ice feature band with urgent seasonal CTA.
5. Residential services as a clearly secondary section with hardscape, softscape, retaining walls, and garden maintenance.
6. Simple process: Assess Property, Build Plan, Maintain & Communicate.
7. Service area / Twin Cities local trust section.
8. Quote form with commercial/residential/snow inquiry selector, accessible labels, phone fallback, and privacy-friendly short fields.
9. Footer with careers, client portal, services, contact, and association proof.

## Interaction Notes

- Sticky mobile bottom action can work well: Call + Quote, with Snow visible seasonally.
- Use service cards for scanning, but keep them utilitarian rather than decorative.
- Use icons sparingly for service categories and proof points; photography should carry the site.
- Forms should be short enough for a property manager to submit on mobile.
- Make every clickable target comfortably tappable and avoid text overlay areas that can wrap into image noise.

## Risks

- Over-indexing on residential landscaping imagery will weaken the commercial reliability pitch.
- Too many CTAs above the fold will blur the conversion path.
- Using only generic stock lawn photos will make the redesign look polished but less credible.
- Current SSL/certificate issue may undermine trust and should be mentioned separately from design.
- If the client portal is important to existing customers, burying it could create friction; keep it visible but visually secondary to new-lead conversion.
