Supporting UX assessment

ProScapes UX scorecard.

The business appears credible and active, but the website leaves meaningful trust and conversion value on the table, especially on mobile.

62 Overall score / 100

Interpretation

ProScapes already has the proof buyers want: service since 1958, commercial capability, 24/7/365 language, memberships, and a client portal. The website should make those strengths faster to understand and easier to act on.

Mobile

Mobile experience

45 / 100

Evidence

  • Current mobile hero text clips off-screen.
  • First impression is visual but not immediately operationally clear.
  • CTA language is generic.

Recommended fix

  • Mobile-first hero with commercial positioning.
  • Direct CTA for commercial quote.
  • Snow/ice CTA close to first screen.

Trust

Trust proof

72 / 100

Evidence

  • Since 1958.
  • 24/7/365 service language.
  • Memberships and associations.
  • Client portal.
  • Commercial and residential service breadth.

Recommended fix

  • Move key value proposition higher.
  • Organize value into a scannable strip.
  • Move commercial reliability and communication higher on the main page.

Conversion

Conversion path

58 / 100

Evidence

  • Site has a "Get Started" CTA.
  • Contact path exists.
  • Buyer intent is not separated cleanly between commercial, snow/ice, and residential.

Recommended fix

  • Primary CTA: Request Commercial Quote.
  • Secondary CTA: Snow & Ice Services.
  • Quote form asks property type and service need.

Technical

Technical foundation

55 / 100

Evidence

  • Could not verify certificates via automated HTTPS fetch.
  • Current mobile layout has hidden navigation.

Recommended fix

  • SSL/certificate cleanup.
  • Responsive layout QA.
  • Local service SEO structure.
  • Compress images and preserve/redirect to critical quote paths.

Content clarity

Content clarity

68 / 100

Evidence

  • Good service content exists.
  • Strong proof points exist.
  • Homepage does not immediately prioritize the commercial buyer.

Recommended fix

  • Reframe homepage around property managers and year-round maintenance.
  • Keep residential services visible but secondary.
  • Use service cards and proof points for faster scanning.
